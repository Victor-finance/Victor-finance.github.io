# Research Overview

My research focuses on understanding the complex dynamics of modern financial markets and corporate decision-making. I employ both theoretical modeling and empirical analysis to investigate how technological innovation, regulatory changes, and behavioral factors shape financial outcomes.

## Current Research Projects

### 1. FinTech Innovation and Traditional Banking
**Status**: Under Review at *Journal of Finance*
**Collaborators**: Prof. [Name], Prof. [Name]

This project examines how the rise of financial technology companies affects traditional banking institutions. Using proprietary data from multiple countries, we analyze changes in market share, pricing strategies, and service offerings. Our findings suggest that traditional banks adapt through strategic partnerships rather than direct competition.

**Key Findings**:
- Traditional banks increase digital investment by 40% in response to FinTech entry
- Customer acquisition costs decrease by 25% through strategic partnerships
- Regulatory frameworks significantly moderate competitive effects

---

### 2. ESG Factors in Credit Risk Assessment
**Status**: Revise & Resubmit at *Review of Financial Studies*
**Funding**: NSF Grant #1234567 ($180,000)

We develop a machine learning framework to incorporate environmental, social, and governance factors into credit risk models. Our approach improves default prediction accuracy while providing insights into the economic value of sustainability practices.

**Methodology**:
- Random Forest and Gradient Boosting algorithms
- Dataset of 50,000+ corporate bonds (2010-2024)
- Cross-validation with out-of-sample testing

**Preliminary Results**:
- 15% improvement in default prediction accuracy
- Environmental factors show strongest predictive power
- Industry heterogeneity in ESG importance

---

### 3. Market Sentiment and Corporate Investment
**Status**: Data Collection Phase
**Timeline**: Submit by December 2025

This project uses natural language processing to analyze earnings call transcripts and their impact on corporate investment decisions. We hypothesize that sentiment-driven investment leads to value destruction in the long term.

**Data Sources**:
- Earnings call transcripts (2000-2024)
- Corporate investment data from Compustat
- Analyst reports and recommendations

## Research Interests by Area

### Corporate Finance
My work in corporate finance focuses on how firms make capital allocation decisions under uncertainty and information asymmetries.

**Recent Publications**:
- "Capital Structure in the Digital Age" (2024) - *Journal of Financial Economics*
- "CEO Overconfidence and Investment Efficiency" (2023) - *Review of Finance*

**Ongoing Projects**:
- Corporate governance in family firms
- The role of institutional investors in ESG adoption
- Cross-border mergers and acquisitions

### Financial Markets
I investigate market microstructure, price discovery mechanisms, and the impact of technology on trading.

**Key Research Questions**:
- How do algorithmic trading strategies affect market liquidity?
- What is the role of social media in price discovery?
- How do regulatory changes impact market efficiency?

**Data and Methods**:
- High-frequency trading data
- Event study methodologies
- Machine learning applications in finance

### Behavioral Finance
My behavioral finance research examines how psychological biases affect investment decisions and market outcomes.

**Current Focus Areas**:
- Investor attention and asset prices
- Herding behavior in institutional investing
- The role of emotions in financial decision-making

**Experimental Work**:
- Laboratory experiments with MBA students
- Field experiments with retail investors
- Survey-based studies of financial advisors
